BioJC

Welcome to BioJC! Bio Journal Club (Bio JC) provides an opportunity for research students and early career researchers to:
keep abreast of new developments in biological sciences.
engage in discussion and interaction.
train presentation skills.
strengthen the ability to critically analyze.

I. [BioJC SEMINAR]

BioJC seminar is held in the Conference Room 208 of the Medical College Research Building A at 6:30 p.m. every Friday, unless otherwise indicated. Our seminar includes following three main forms:

Rotation talks:

Core members take turns to present and discuss interesting articles from high impact scientific journals every week. 40 mins for presentation, 20 mins for discussion.

Workshop:

A meeting with a particular topic to engage in intensive discussion, usually with two speakers, 30 mins for each speaker and 15 mins for discussion.

Invited presentation:

Speakers who published a high-quality paper will be invited to give a keynote speech. 50 mins for invited speakers and 20 mins for questions.

II.[THE SPEAKER]

The articles you choose should be specific enough to gain interests.
Prepare your presentation carefully.
Give a notice a week ahead in our Bio JC WeChat group, and explain why you choose the paper. (Express in English)
Upload your PPT and supplemental materials with the format of PDF in the 126 Yunfile (Account：biojournalclub@126.com Password：BioJC20180826) after our meeting.

III.[DURING THE PRESENTATION]

Give your presentation in English.
Try not always to read the manuscripts you prepared.
Please use PPT to present the paper.
Provide your audience with the necessary information in the background section, as well as previous studies by early researchers.
Try to make sure you understand the technical details of the paper in order to describe the study clearly.
Try to emphasize the keywords with pointer during your presentation, if needed.
Give a short summary of the paper before closing your presentation, and then opens it up for discussion.
More info: Deployment

IV.[PPT]

Show clear and logical slides to your audience.
Use words and phrases correctly.
Add a page number on each slide.
Add Chinese definition if necessary.
Quote all the information correctly.
Give a brief summary when a section finished.

V.[AUDIENCE AND QUESTIONS]

Arrive on time.
Do not disrupt the speaker by putting your question during a section.
Think carefully before asking.
Describe your question in English as much as you can.
Avoid too many questions only associated with your own project.

VI.[MEMBERS]

Bio JC is made up of core members (fixed) and mobile members (unfixed, free participation). The core members are a group of people who are willing to share a rotation talk.
Bio JC is not for the core members only, everybody could join a meeting, and a core member could be selected from the newcomers, or recommended by other core members.
